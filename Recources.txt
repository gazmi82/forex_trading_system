The 5 Core Drivers of EUR/USD
1. FED vs ECB Interest Rate Differential
   Most powerful driver of EUR/USD direction
   Fed hawkish + ECB dovish = EUR/USD falls
   Fed dovish + ECB hawkish = EUR/USD rises
   Current differential drives weeks/months of trend

2. DXY (US Dollar Index)
   EUR is 57.6% of the DXY basket
   DXY rising = EUR/USD falling (almost always)
   Always check DXY before any EUR/USD trade
   They move in near-perfect inverse correlation

3. Economic Data Surprises
   US: NFP, CPI, GDP, Retail Sales
   EU: PMI, German IFO, ECB statements
   Stronger US data = USD strengthens = EUR/USD falls
   Stronger EU data = EUR strengthens = EUR/USD rises

4. Risk Sentiment
   Risk-on (stocks rising) = EUR/USD tends to rise
   Risk-off (fear in markets) = USD strengthens = falls
   Watch S&P500 direction as a confirmation tool

5. COT Positioning
   EUR futures on CME show institutional bias
   Commercials heavily long EUR = bullish signal
   Released every Friday 3:30PM EST — check weekly
EUR/USD Session Behavior (ICT)
Asian Session (8PM–4AM EST):
  → EUR/USD builds a range (high and low)
  → This range is the liquidity target for London
  → Note the Asian high and Asian low every day

London Kill Zone (3AM–4AM EST):
  → Price sweeps Asian session high OR low first
  → This is the liquidity grab before the real move
  → After the sweep → trade the reversal
  → Most powerful EUR/USD setup of the day

NY Kill Zone (8AM–10AM EST):
  → Either continues London move OR reverses it
  → NFP and CPI always drop at 8:30AM here
  → Best session for catching momentum continuations

London Close (10AM–12PM EST):
  → Profit taking by London banks
  → Often reverses the London move partially
  → Good for counter-trend scalps
EUR/USD Key Levels to Always Track
Track these every single week:

Weekly levels:
→ Previous week high (PWH)
→ Previous week low (PWL)
→ Current week open

Daily levels:
→ Previous day high (PDH)
→ Previous day low (PDL)
→ Current day open

Institutional levels:
→ Round numbers: 1.0500, 1.0600, 1.0700, 1.0800, 1.0900, 1.1000
→ These act as magnets and rejection zones

Monthly levels:
→ Previous month high/low
→ Current month open price
