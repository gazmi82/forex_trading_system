## PHASE 1 — Independent Confluence Scoring
**Score impact: 38 → 50**

### The Problem
The confluence score (0-100) is generated by Claude inside the analysis.
Claude scores its own work. This is circular — the same model that
produces the signal grades it. A score of 85 from Claude has no proven
relationship to actual win rate. You cannot use it to make statistical
decisions.

### Item 1.1 — Mechanical Confluence Calculator
**New file:** `app/analysis/confluence_scorer.py`

Build a function `calculate_confluence(market_data, signal) -> dict`
that implements the scoring table from the system prompt in deterministic
Python code:

```
TREND ALIGNMENT (max 15):
  +15  Weekly + Daily + 4H all ALIGNED
  +10  Daily + 4H aligned, Weekly NEUTRAL
  +5   Only 4H shows direction

ICT (max 70):
  +20  Valid Order Block, trend-aligned, not mitigated
  +15  Fair Value Gap at entry zone, not fully filled
  +15  Liquidity sweep in last 48H
  +10  Price in DISCOUNT (BUY) or PREMIUM (SELL)
  +10  OTE Fibonacci zone (62-79%)

CLASSICAL TA (max 25):
  +10  RSI divergence on 4H or 1H
  +10  ADX > 25
  +5   EMA alignment

FUNDAMENTAL (max 40):
  +15  Rate differential supports direction
  +10  DXY confirms
  +10  COT confirms
  +5   No high-impact news in next 4 hours
```

Returns:
```python
{
  "confluence_score": 78,
  "direction_implied": "BUY",
  "component_scores": {
      "trend_alignment": 10, "order_block": 20, "fvg": 15,
      "liquidity_sweep": 0, "premium_discount": 10, "ote": 0,
      "rsi": 0, "adx": 10, "ema": 5,
      "rate_differential": 15, "dxy": 10, "cot": 0, "news_clear": 5
  }
}
```

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Same `market_data` always returns the same score (deterministic).
- Score range matches system prompt thresholds (85+ STRONG, 65-84 MODERATE).
- All 13 components are individually testable with unit tests.

### Item 1.2 — Wire Mechanical Score into Validator
**File:** `app/analysis/agent.py` (`_validate_signal`)

- Call `calculate_confluence(market_data, signal)` after Claude returns.
- Replace `signal["confluence_score"]` with the mechanical score.
- Store Claude's value as `signal["claude_confluence_score"]` for comparison.
- Block trade execution if mechanical score < 65, regardless of Claude.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- A trade where Claude reports 80 but mechanical score is 58 is blocked.
- Both scores appear in every saved `signal_*.json`.

---

### Item 1.3 — Score Calibration Log
**File:** `app/analysis/agent.py` (`_log_analysis`)

Log the delta between Claude's score and the mechanical score to
`logs/score_calibration.jsonl` per analysis:
```json
{
  "timestamp": "...", "session": "London Kill Zone",
  "claude_score": 82, "mechanical_score": 71, "delta": -11,
  "direction": "BUY", "outcome": null
}
```
After a closed trade, update the matching entry with `"outcome"`.
After 50 entries, run a correlation: do higher mechanical scores
produce higher win rates? This validates or adjusts scoring weights.

### Item 1.4 — Enforce Weekly Loss Limit
**Files:** `app/analysis/agent.py`, `app/execution/trade_executor.py`

Calculate weekly P&L from `closed_trades.jsonl` entries where `date`
falls in the current ISO week. If weekly loss >= `max_weekly_loss` (5%),
block all new trades for the rest of the week.

- Same pattern as daily loss enforcement — project new trade before deciding.
- Block resets at Monday 00:00 UTC.
- Both agent validator and executor pre-trade checks must enforce this.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- After 5% weekly loss, signal direction forced to NEUTRAL.
- `BLOCKED: Weekly loss limit reached` appears in `validator_overrides`.
- Unit tested the same way daily loss was tested.

## PHASE 2 — Prove the Edge Exists (Backtesting)
**Score impact: 50 → 60**

### The Problem
The system has never been tested against historical data. Every demo
trade is a blind experiment. Without historical validation you cannot
distinguish a real edge from random noise or survivorship bias.

### Item 2.1 — Historical Data Loader
**New file:** `app/backtesting/data_loader.py`

Class `HistoricalDataLoader`:
- Fetches and caches OANDA OHLCV candles for any date range and
  granularity (D, H4, H1, M15).
- Stores locally in `backtest_data/` as CSV to avoid re-fetching.
- Supports multi-timeframe alignment: given a timestamp T on H1,
  returns correct H4 and Daily context with zero look-ahead bias.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Load 3 years of EUR/USD H1 data in under 30 seconds from cache.
- Multi-timeframe slices contain no data timestamped after T.

---

### Item 2.2 — Signal Replay Engine
**New file:** `app/backtesting/signal_replayer.py`

Walk forward through every Kill Zone window in the historical data:
- London KZ: 03:00-04:00 EST on weekdays
- NY KZ: 08:00-10:00 EST on weekdays
- London Close: 10:00-12:00 EST on weekdays

At each window, build `market_data` from historical candles (same
structure as `MarketDataBuilder` produces live). Run
`IndicatorCalculator`, `MarketStructureAnalyzer`, and the mechanical
confluence scorer (Item 1.1). Record one `BacktestSignal` per window.

**Critical:** Do NOT call the Claude API. The backtester uses only the
mechanical scorer. Claude is non-deterministic and costs money.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Replays 2 years of Kill Zone windows with zero look-ahead.
- Completes in under 5 minutes.
- Produces one record per window: timestamp, session, direction,
  confluence score, entry zone, SL, TP1, TP2.

---

### Item 2.3 — Outcome Simulator
**New file:** `app/backtesting/outcome_simulator.py`

Given a `BacktestSignal`, walk forward through subsequent candles and
determine the outcome following the live executor's rules exactly:
- Entry fills when price touches the entry zone midpoint.
- TP1 hit → close `tp1_close_percent`, move SL to entry.
- After TP1 → apply ATR-based trailing stop.
- Time stop → close at session's configured hours if < -0.5R.
- Final close at TP2 or SL.

Output: `pnl_r`, `outcome` (WIN/LOSS/BREAKEVEN/PARTIAL_WIN),
`hold_time_hours`, `close_reason`.

Output format matches `closed_trades.jsonl` so the same edge report
can read both live and backtest results.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Outcome logic matches live executor rule for rule.
- Partial close (TP1 hit, runner stopped at entry) modelled correctly.

---

### Item 2.4 — Backtest Performance Report
**New file:** `app/backtesting/report.py`

Read simulated closed trades and produce:
- Total trades, win rate, avg R, expectancy.
- Breakdown by session and by confluence score bucket.
- Max consecutive losses, max drawdown, profit factor.
- Saved to `backtest_results/report.json`.

**Minimum thresholds to proceed to live trading:**
- Expectancy > 0 per trade.
- Profit factor > 1.3.
- Win rate shown per session — remove any session with negative expectancy.

## PHASE 3 — Performance Intelligence
**Score impact: 60 → 67**

### The Problem
Pattern tags, setup grades, and root causes are logged per trade, but
nothing aggregates them. After 50 demo trades you need answers to:
"What is my win rate on ob_entry + post_sweep + London Kill Zone setups?"

### Item 3.1 — Edge Database Aggregator
**New file:** `app/performance/edge_report.py`

Function `generate_edge_report(closed_trades_path) -> dict` that
aggregates by:
- Session: win rate, avg R, expectancy, sample count.
- Setup grade: does A outperform C statistically?
- Root cause: what % of CORRECT_PROCESS_ADVERSE_OUTCOME recover?
- Pattern tag combinations: win rate per tag and per pair of tags.
- Mechanical score bucket: <70, 70-79, 80-89, 90+.
- Claude score vs mechanical score: which predicts outcomes better?

Only report groups with >= 10 trades to avoid misleading small samples.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- After 100 demo trades, identifies at least one condition to add or
  remove from the scoring system based on evidence.
- Identifies the session with the highest expectancy.

## PHASE 5 — ICT Implementation Quality
**Score impact: 72 → 75**

### The Problem
Order Block detection marks every valid OB as `(4H, valid)` permanently.
In reality, an OB is invalidated once price trades back through it
(mitigated). FVG detection marks gaps as `unfilled` but never updates
when price partially or fully fills them.

### Item 5.1 — Order Block Mitigation
**File:** `app/analysis/market_analysis.py` (`_find_order_block`)

After identifying the OB candle, walk forward through subsequent candles:
- Bullish OB mitigated: a close below `ob_low`.
- Bearish OB mitigated: a close above `ob_high`.

Return: `"1.0820–1.0835 (4H, valid, 3 candles ago)"` or
`"1.0820–1.0835 (4H, MITIGATED)"`.

A MITIGATED OB receives zero confluence points in the mechanical scorer.

---

### Item 5.2 — FVG Fill Status
**File:** `app/analysis/market_analysis.py` (`_find_fvg`)

After identifying the gap, walk forward and check if subsequent candles
have traded into it:
- Unfilled: no candle has entered the gap range.
- Partial: a candle entered but did not close through.
- Filled: a candle closed through the entire gap.

Fully filled FVG = zero points. Partial fill = half points.

---

### Item 5.3 — Displacement Candle Validation
**File:** `app/analysis/market_analysis.py` (`_find_order_block`)

The current `strong_move` check requires the next 3 candles to move 2×
the OB range. Refine to also require:
- Displacement candle (first after OB) closes in the top/bottom 25%
  of its range in the direction of the move.
- Displacement candle body >= 1.5× ATR(14) of the lookback period.

This removes OBs preceded by slow, grinding moves that lack
institutional conviction.

## What 75 Does Not Mean

A score of 75 means the system has:
- A proven, repeatable process.
- Validated positive expectancy on 2+ years of historical data.
- Evidence-based filtering of which setups to take.
- Trade management rules calibrated to actual session behavior.

It does not mean:
- The system is ready for live capital. That decision belongs to Month 12
  after demo results match or exceed backtest expectations.
- The system works in all market regimes. A 2-year backtest gives
  reasonable confidence; covering a true risk-off shock (2020, 2022)
  would give more.
- Claude's analysis is the edge. The mechanical scorer is the gate.
  Claude provides reasoning context and lesson generation — those are
  its appropriate roles.

The path from 75 to 90 is earned through live performance:
6 months of real capital where demo results are replicated on a
real account. That cannot be engineered — it must be observed.
