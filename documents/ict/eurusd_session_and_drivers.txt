EUR/USD Specific Knowledge — Session Behavior, Core Drivers, and Key Levels

=============================================================================
THE 5 CORE DRIVERS OF EUR/USD
=============================================================================

1. FED vs ECB Interest Rate Differential
   Most powerful driver of EUR/USD direction.
   Fed hawkish + ECB dovish = EUR/USD falls.
   Fed dovish + ECB hawkish = EUR/USD rises.
   Current rate differential drives weeks and months of trend direction.
   Always identify which central bank is more hawkish before forming a bias.

2. DXY (US Dollar Index)
   EUR is 57.6% of the DXY basket — the largest single component.
   DXY rising = EUR/USD falling (near-perfect inverse correlation).
   DXY falling = EUR/USD rising.
   Always check DXY direction before any EUR/USD trade.
   DXY and EUR/USD move in near-perfect inverse correlation at all times.

3. Economic Data Surprises
   US data: NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls), CPI, GDP, Retail Sales.
   EU data: PMI (Purchasing Managers Index), German IFO, ECB statements.
   Stronger US data = USD strengthens = EUR/USD falls.
   Stronger EU data = EUR strengthens = EUR/USD rises.
   A data beat does not guarantee a move — the prior expectation matters most.

4. Risk Sentiment
   Risk-on environment (stocks rising, VIX falling) = EUR/USD tends to rise.
   Risk-off environment (fear in markets) = USD strengthens = EUR/USD falls.
   Watch S&P500 direction as a confirmation tool for EUR/USD bias.
   USD is the primary global safe-haven currency.

5. COT Positioning (Commitments of Traders)
   EUR futures on CME show institutional long/short bias.
   Commercials heavily long EUR = bullish signal for EUR/USD.
   Large speculators extremely short = contrarian bullish signal (crowded short).
   COT report released every Friday at 3:30PM EST — check weekly.
   Source: cftc.gov — Disaggregated Futures Only report, EUR futures.

EUR/USD SESSION BEHAVIOR (ICT SMART MONEY CONCEPTS)
=============================================================================

Asian Session (8PM–4AM EST):
   EUR/USD builds a consolidation range during low liquidity.
   The Asian high and Asian low define the liquidity on both sides.
   This range is the primary liquidity target for the London session.
   Note the Asian high and Asian low every single trading day.
   Do not trade during Asian session for EUR/USD — observe only.

London Kill Zone (3AM–4AM EST):
   Most important EUR/USD setup of the entire trading day.
   Price sweeps the Asian session high OR the Asian session low first.
   This initial move is a liquidity grab — it hunts retail stop losses.
   After the sweep of Asian highs or lows, trade the reversal.
   The true London direction is the opposite of the initial sweep direction.
   Entry confirmation: price sweeps, then forms a strong rejection candle.
   Best entry: inside the Order Block left before the liquidity sweep.

NY Kill Zone (8AM–10AM EST):
   Either continues the London move OR reverses it at a key level.
   NFP, CPI, and all major US economic data always drops at 8:30AM EST here.
   Best session for catching momentum continuations after London.
   NY Open often retests the London session Order Block for entry.
   During high-impact news, widen stops or wait 15 minutes for volatility.

London Close (10AM–12PM EST):
   London banks begin taking profits on their London session positions.
   Often creates a partial reversal of the London move.
   Good session for counter-trend scalps or exiting positions early.
   Not ideal for new position entries — liquidity decreasing.

Asian Session Dead Zone (12PM–8PM EST):
   Avoid trading. Low liquidity. Choppy, directionless price action.
   Manipulation and stop hunts common without follow-through.
   Use this time to plan next session trades and review charts.

EUR/USD KEY LEVELS TO TRACK EVERY WEEK
=============================================================================

Weekly Levels (most important — set every Sunday):
   Previous Week High (PWH) — major resistance target and liquidity pool.
   Previous Week Low (PWL) — major support target and liquidity pool.
   Current Week Open — institutional reference price for the week.
   Price is bullish for the week if above week open, bearish if below.

Daily Levels (set every day at 5PM EST market open):
   Previous Day High (PDH) — daily resistance and buy-side liquidity.
   Previous Day Low (PDL) — daily support and sell-side liquidity.
   Current Day Open — intraday bias reference.

Institutional Round Number Levels (permanent):
   1.0500 — major psychological support/resistance.
   1.0600 — major psychological support/resistance.
   1.0700 — major psychological support/resistance.
   1.0800 — major psychological support/resistance.
   1.0900 — major psychological support/resistance.
   1.1000 — major psychological support/resistance.
   These round numbers act as price magnets and strong rejection zones.
   Price frequently stalls, reverses, or accelerates through these levels.

Monthly Levels (check at start of each month):
   Previous Month High — major buy-side liquidity above.
   Previous Month Low — major sell-side liquidity below.
   Current Month Open — monthly bias reference (above = bullish, below = bearish).

=============================================================================
ICT CONCEPTS SPECIFIC TO EUR/USD
=============================================================================

Order Blocks:
   The last opposing candle before a strong impulse move.
   Bullish OB: last bearish candle before a strong bullish move.
   Bearish OB: last bullish candle before a strong bearish move.
   Price frequently returns to the Order Block zone for re-entry.
   An Order Block is invalidated when price closes through it.

Fair Value Gaps (FVG / Imbalance):
   A three-candle pattern where candle 1 high and candle 3 low do not overlap.
   Bullish FVG: buying imbalance — price likely returns to fill the gap.
   Bearish FVG: selling imbalance — price likely returns to fill the gap.
   FVGs act as high-probability entry zones when price returns to them.

Premium and Discount Zones:
   Use a swing high and swing low to define the range.
   Equilibrium = 50% of the range (Fibonacci 0.5 level).
   Premium = above equilibrium. Only sell in premium zones.
   Discount = below equilibrium. Only buy in discount zones.
   Never buy at the top of a range or sell at the bottom.

Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):
   The 62–79% Fibonacci retracement zone of any impulse move.
   Highest probability entry zone for continuation of the trend.
   Combine OTE with an Order Block for maximum confluence.

Liquidity Sweeps:
   Price targets equal highs (buy-side liquidity — retail buy stops).
   Price targets equal lows (sell-side liquidity — retail sell stops).
   After sweeping liquidity, price reverses sharply in the true direction.
   A liquidity sweep followed by a strong rejection = high confidence setup.

=============================================================================
EUR/USD TRADING RULES DERIVED FROM ICT CONCEPTS
=============================================================================

Rule 1: Always identify higher timeframe bias first (Weekly → Daily → 4H → 1H).
Rule 2: Only trade in the direction of the Daily and Weekly trend.
Rule 3: Wait for a liquidity sweep before entering a position.
Rule 4: Enter only in discount (for longs) or premium (for shorts).
Rule 5: Enter on Order Blocks or Fair Value Gaps after a sweep.
Rule 6: London Kill Zone (3–4AM EST) is the highest probability window.
Rule 7: NY Kill Zone (8–10AM EST) is the second-highest probability window.
Rule 8: Never trade the Asian session for EUR/USD entries.
Rule 9: Avoid trading 30 minutes before major news events (NFP, CPI, FOMC, ECB).
Rule 10: If all timeframes are not aligned, do not take the trade.
